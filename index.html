Sophia Longo

I'm currently a Stanford student pursuing an MS in Biomedical Data Science and graduated with BS in Human Biology with Honors (Cancer Biology & Therapeutic Intervention) in June 2024. I conduct research with the Khavari Laboratory and the Stanford Skin Innovation and Interventional Research Group (SIIRG). I am an aspiring physician-scientist! My research interests lie at the intersection of cancer immunology, biomedical informatics, and precision oncology.

Feel free to reach out to me: sklongo@stanford.edu

Research

  • In the Khavari Lab, the project I am currently working on is a continuation of my undergraduate honors thesis: "Identification of non-coding genetic variants that disrupt cis-regulatory mechanisms of antigen presentation in cancers and autoimmune diseases" (awarded Firestone Medal for Excellence in Undergraduate Research)
  • First-authored a review article on “Integrating single-cell and spatial transcriptomics to elucidate intercellular tissue dynamics” (Longo, Guo, Ji, & Khavari, Nature Reviews Genetics 2021)
  • Partnered with lab member to build interactive “Human Skin Browser” of single-cell RNA-seq and spatial transcriptomic data of human skin (included as co-author in Thrane et al. Journal of Investigative Dermatology 2023)
  • With the Stanford Skin Innovation and Interventional Research Group (SIIRG), I am analyzing and integrating data collected from a total body skin imaging device with EHR and patient survey data.  Our ultimate goal is to clinically implement device to optimize removal of potentially cancerous lesions while minimizing excessive biopsies.
